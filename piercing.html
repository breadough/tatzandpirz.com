Preparation and Aftercare

Things to do before and after having a piercing

Piercing Preparation Checklist

Over the age of 18.

Not under the influence of alcohol or drugs.

Not pregnant or nursing.

Have a good rest and eat a meal before the session, so you won't feel dizzy.

Have your body to be pierced clean.

Avoid drinking products with caffeine before the session.

Ear Piercing Aftercare:

Always wash your hand before touching the newly pierced body part.

Regulary wash your new pircieng with soap and water or saline solution, carefully do this once a day.

Leave the earings in your new pircieng until it is fully healed, removing your initial earings too early may cause the piercing to close.

Cartilage piercing will require a bit more time to heal than a lobe piercing.

Tie your hair as much as possible to prevent it from snagging your jewelry.

For navel piercing, it is advisable to sleep on you back to prevent the jewelry from moving.

Do not forget to consult and update your piercer with the condition..

Navel Piercing Aftercare

Navel Piercing Aftercare:

Keep it Clean: Gently clean your navel piercing twice a day with saline solution or mild soap and water.

Hands Off: Refrain from touching, twisting, or playing with your new piercing to avoid introducing bacteria and prevent irritation.

Avoid Submerging: Avoid swimming in pools, hot tubs, or natural bodies of water during the healing process to prevent infection.

Clothing Considerations: Wear loose-fitting clothing to prevent friction and irritation around the piercing area.

Pat Dry: After cleaning, pat the piercing dry with a clean disposable paper towel or let it air dry.

Avoid Ointments: Do not use any ointments, creams, or oils on the piercing as they may trap bacteria and slow down the healing process.

Sleep Position: If possible, sleep on your back to prevent unnecessary pressure on the piercing.

Be Patient: Healing times vary, but it typically takes 6 to 12 months for a navel piercing to fully heal. Be patient and follow the aftercare instructions diligently.

Watch for Signs of Infection: Keep an eye out for any signs of infection, such as increased redness, swelling, pus, or severe pain. If you suspect an infection, seek professional advice immediately.

Lastly, Update your artist with the status.

Piercing Bump Aftercare:

Do NOT pop the bump. This could cause an infection and end up with a scar.

Hold a pad soaked in warm water against it once a day for 5 mins.

It is advisable to clean your new piercing once or twice a day.

Avoid touching your piercing(aside from cleaning) and tie your hair as much as possible to prevent it from snagging your jewelry.

Condition should improve and the bump will eventually subside on its own.
